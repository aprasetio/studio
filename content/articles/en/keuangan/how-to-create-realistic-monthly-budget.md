## Why Does a Monthly Budget Matter?

Many people find their paycheck disappearing without knowing where it went. Without a budget, impulse spending happens easily and long-term financial goals become hard to reach.

Budgeting doesn't mean living frugally. A budget is a tool to **make sure your money works according to your priorities**.

## Step 1: Calculate Your Net Income

List all of your income sources for the month:
- Base salary (after taxes and deductions)
- Side income
- Dividends, royalties, or other passive income

Use figures that **are guaranteed to land in your account**, not projections.

## Step 2: List All Fixed Expenses

Fixed expenses are those that stay the same every month:
- Mortgage or rent payments
- Vehicle loan payments
- Insurance premiums
- Internet bills and subscriptions

Add them all up — this is the minimum amount you need to set aside.

## Step 3: Estimate Variable Expenses

Variable expenses change from month to month, for example:
- Groceries and household supplies
- Transportation (fuel, parking)
- Dining out
- Entertainment and shopping

Review your transaction history from the last 3 months to get a realistic estimate.

## Step 4: Apply the 50/30/20 Rule

A popular method for dividing your budget:

| Category | Percentage | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Needs | 50% | Rent, food, transportation |
| Wants | 30% | Entertainment, dining out |
| Savings/Investments | 20% | Emergency fund, investments |

Adjust these percentages to fit your situation. The key principle: **savings should be the first expense, not what's left over**.

## Step 5: Review and Adjust

The best budget is one you actually use. Do a quick weekly review to:
- Make sure spending is on track with your plan
- Adjust if unexpected needs come up
- Celebrate small wins

## Tips for Budget Success

1. **Start simple** — you don't need overly detailed categories
2. **Automate your savings** — transfer to a savings account at the start of the month
3. **Build an emergency fund** — aim for at least 3 months of expenses
4. **Use the right tools** — apps or spreadsheets make tracking much easier

With consistency, budgeting will feel more and more natural as the months go by.
