## What Are Syariah Stocks?

Syariah stocks are equity securities that comply with Islamic law (syariah) principles. In Indonesia, the Daftar Efek Syariah (DES — List of Syariah Securities) is published by the OJK (Financial Services Authority) twice a year, containing hundreds of stocks that have passed rigorous screening.

A stock qualifies as syariah-compliant if it meets two primary criteria:

**1. Halal Business Activities**
The company must not operate in areas prohibited by syariah, such as:
- Gambling and games of chance
- Production or distribution of alcoholic beverages
- Riba (interest-based finance)
- Entertainment prohibited under syariah

**2. Healthy Financial Ratios**
- Interest-bearing debt to total assets ratio ≤ 45%
- Non-halal income to total revenue ratio ≤ 10%

## Why Choose Syariah Stocks?

Investing in syariah stocks is not just about religious compliance. There are tangible financial benefits:

1. **Rigorous Screening = Healthier Businesses** — The DES screening process indirectly filters out companies carrying excessive debt
2. **Suitable for Conservative Investors** — Debt ratio limits make the portfolio more stable
3. **Growing Ecosystem** — Syariah mutual funds, sukuk (Islamic bonds), and syariah ETFs continue to expand

## How to Start Investing in Syariah Stocks

### Step 1: Open a Syariah Securities Account
Choose a broker that offers a dedicated syariah account. Most major brokers in Indonesia now provide this feature.

### Step 2: Study the Latest DES
Download the most recent Daftar Efek Syariah from the OJK website. The list is updated every May and November.

### Step 3: Analyze Before You Buy
Don't buy a stock simply because it is syariah-compliant. Always analyze:
- **Fundamentals**: financial statements, business prospects
- **Technicals**: price trends, support and resistance levels
- **Valuation**: is the current price fair?

### Step 4: Diversify
Don't put everything into a single stock. Spread your investment across multiple sectors.

## Tips for Beginners

- **Start with what you know** — choose companies whose products or services you use in daily life
- **Be consistent** — regular investing matters more than finding the perfect entry point
- **Be patient** — stock investing is a long-term game
- **Keep learning** — markets are always changing; knowledge is your most valuable asset
